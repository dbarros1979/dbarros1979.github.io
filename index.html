Research Interests

My main academic focus is the intersection of Bioinformatics, Computational Oncology, and Artificial Intelligence.

Biomarker Discovery

Discovery of genes, proteins, and microRNAs as biomarkers for cancer diagnosis and prognosis.

Probabilistic Models

DNA/RNA motif analysis using statistical models including Gibbs Sampling and profile matrices.

Sequence Analysis

Sequence alignment, scoring, and statistical evaluation for genomic data.

ML for Cancer Classification

Machine learning models for cancer classification, prediction, and personalized treatment.

Long-term Goal

To contribute tools, datasets, and predictive models that improve early detection and personalized treatment for cancer patients.

Selected Projects

DNA Motif Search Toolkit

Python implementations of motif discovery algorithms, including: Consensus calculation, Profile matrices, Pseudocounts, GreedyMotifSearch, Gibbs Sampling, ProfileMostProbableKmer, and probability scoring.

Assembler Emulator (riscpy)

A functional minimalist ISA emulator implemented from scratch for educational purposes, bridging hardware theory and software simulation.

Computational Oncology Tools

Early-stage Jupyter notebooks and scripts experimenting with biomarker extraction, differential expression, and microRNA analysis for cancer research.
