About Me

My name is Daniel D'Angelo Resende Barros. I am a software engineer and researcher dedicated to making Computer Science education accessible to everyone. My work bridges the gap between technical software engineering and pedagogical inclusivity.


I focus on designing tools and curricula that support neurodiverse learners and students with disabilities, ensuring that the next generation of developers represents the diversity of our society.

Research Interests

My academic focus is on Broadening Participation in Computing (BPC) through intelligent tools and inclusive frameworks.

Accessible IDEs

Investigating how Integrated Development Environments can be optimized for screen readers and neurodiverse cognitive styles.

Neurodiversity in CS

Developing teaching strategies that leverage the strengths of students with ADHD, Autism, and Dyslexia in programming tasks.

Automated Scaffolding

Using AI to provide personalized hints and error messages that reduce cognitive load for novice programmers.
